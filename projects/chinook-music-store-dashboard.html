Chinook Music Store: Sales Analytics Dashboard

  • PostgreSQL
  • Power BI
  • DAX
  • Excel
View dashboard →

Dashboard

A single-page executive dashboard grouping all five business categories together with consistent styling: genre and country revenue, employee sales performance, track versus album purchasing, and customer churn.

Problem statement

Built a business intelligence dashboard for a digital music store, answering five core business questions: which genres and countries generate the most revenue, how sales support employees perform, whether customers buy complete albums or individual tracks, and which customers are at risk of churning.

Tools used

  • PostgreSQL - wrote the SQL (joins, CTEs, window functions, multi-CTE set comparisons) that answered each business question against the Chinook sample database.
  • Power BI - connected to each SQL query as a custom data source and built DAX measures for interactivity.
  • Excel - built a pivot table fallback version of the dashboard.

Process

  1. Wrote and verified 5 SQL queries directly against a PostgreSQL instance (Chinook sample database), each answering one business category.
  2. Used each query as a custom SQL data source in Power BI (not raw table imports): SQL handled the extraction and aggregation logic, while Power BI and DAX handled interactivity.
  3. Built DAX measures for ranking (top genre/country by revenue), percentage-of-total calculations, and churn rate.
  4. Designed a single-page executive dashboard (not multi-tab) grouping all 5 categories together with consistent styling.

Key findings

  • Genre revenue: Rock dominates at 35.5% of total, with Jazz, Metal, and Alternative & Punk trailing well behind.
  • Country revenue: The USA leads at 22.5% of total, with Canada and France trailing.
  • Track versus album purchases: 97.8% of purchase revenue comes from individual track purchases rather than complete albums, reflecting the well-documented industry shift away from album-bundle buying once digital storefronts unbundled purchases.
  • Customer churn: the churn rate sits at 67.8% (40 of 59 customers inactive for 3+ months relative to the dataset's own timeline), suggesting most of the customer base are one-time or infrequent buyers.
