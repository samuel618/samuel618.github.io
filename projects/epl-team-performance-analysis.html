EPL Team Performance Analysis

  • Python
  • pandas
  • matplotlib
  • Data Visualization
View repo on GitHub → Open notebook in new tab →

Problem statement

League tables show who finished where, but not why. This project digs into two full Premier League seasons (2023-24 and 2024-25, 760 matches across 23 clubs) to answer ten concrete questions about what actually separates strong sides from weak ones: home and away form, goals scored and conceded, clean sheets, both-teams-to-score and over-2.5-goals rates, and recent form.

Tools used

  • Python - for the full analysis workflow.
  • pandas - for data cleaning, feature engineering, and aggregation.
  • matplotlib - for the ranking and rate charts.
  • Jupyter Notebook - as the working environment.

Process

  1. Loaded the raw match-level results for both seasons (380 matches each) and combined them into a single 760-match dataset.
  2. Checked data quality: confirmed there were no missing values across the six core columns, no duplicate rows, and that the full-time result column only contained valid values (H, D, A).
  3. Cleaned and standardized the data by keeping only the relevant columns, renaming them to consistent snake_case names, and casting dates and goal counts to the correct types.
  4. Engineered per-match features: total goals, both-teams-to-score and over-2.5-goals flags, home/away win-draw-loss and points, clean sheets, and failed-to-score flags.
  5. Aggregated matches into home and away summaries per team, merged them into one overall team summary, and validated the merged totals by spot-checking three teams' season figures by hand.
  6. Ranked and charted teams across each metric, including points, goal difference, home-vs-away point gap, BTTS rate, over-2.5 rate, clean sheet rate, and failed-to-score rate, to compare the top and bottom of the table.

Key findings

  • Best home record: Liverpool, with 94 points from home matches alone.
  • Worst away record: Sheffield United, with just 6 points on the road.
  • Most goals scored per match: Liverpool, at 2.26.
  • Most goals conceded per match: Sheffield United, at 2.74.
  • Highest clean sheet rate: Arsenal, at 40.8%.
  • Highest failed-to-score rate: Leicester, at 44.7%.
  • Highest BTTS rate: Luton, at 81.6%.
  • Highest over-2.5-goals rate: Sheffield United, at 71.1%.
  • Best recent form: Brighton, 13 points from their last 5 matches (W-D-W-W-W); worst: Sheffield United, 0 points (five straight losses).
  • Largest home-vs-away point gap: Newcastle, at 30 points.

Together, these point to three themes. First, top-table success was attack-driven: Liverpool led the overall table by just 3 points over Arsenal on the back of the league's top scoring rate, while Arsenal's title challenge rested on the division's best defense (highest clean sheet rate, best goal difference at +97). Second, bottom-table struggles were rarely down to a single flaw. Sheffield United combined the worst away form, the most goals conceded per match, the highest rate of high-scoring games against them, and the joint-worst goal difference (-69), a pattern of compounding weaknesses rather than one fixable problem. Third, home advantage was a real differentiator and varied a lot by club: Newcastle's 30-point home-vs-away swing was the largest in the data, showing that where a match was played mattered as much as who was playing.
